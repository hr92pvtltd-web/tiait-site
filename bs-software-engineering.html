Program Overview

The BS Software Engineering program at Tarar Institute of AI & Technology prepares students to build software the way modern technology teams do. Alongside strong programming foundations, the degree focuses on the full software development lifecycle — requirements engineering, design and architecture, quality assurance, testing, DevOps and project management — using agile methods and industry-standard tools.

Students learn by doing: building web and mobile applications, working in teams on real projects, earning professional certifications and completing an industrial internship and a capstone final year project. Graduates emerge as disciplined engineers who can design, deliver and maintain dependable, large-scale software for employers in Pakistan and abroad.

Eligibility Criteria

Who can apply? Applicants who have completed 12 years of education (or will do so in the current admission session) are eligible, subject to the following:

Intermediate (Pre-Engineering / ICS) or equivalent Minimum 50% marks Mathematics required at intermediate level A-Levels / foreign qualifications with IBCC equivalence

Per HEC rules, candidates with qualifications other than SSC/HSSC may be required to obtain an IBCC equivalence certificate.

Admission Test & Selection

Admission is based on academic record, an admission test and an interview. The test assesses analytical reasoning, mathematics and basic English, and is held at the TIAIT campus. Final merit combines your previous academic results with test performance. Our admissions team will guide you through test dates and preparation.

Fee Structure

TIAIT offers competitive, transparent fees with no hidden charges and flexible, semester-wise installment plans. Merit and need-based scholarships are available for deserving students. For the current per-semester fee for BS Software Engineering and details of financial support, please contact the admissions office.
